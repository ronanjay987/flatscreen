Screen Size and Resolution

Screen size, measured diagonally in inches, determines the TV's physical size. Larger screens are ideal for larger rooms and group viewing, while smaller screens are better suited for smaller spaces or personal use.

Resolution, referring to the number of pixels on the screen, directly impacts image clarity and sharpness. Higher resolutions like 4K and 8K offer greater detail and a sharper picture.

Display Technology

LED TVs offer good picture quality, energy efficiency, and affordability.

QLED (Quantum Dot LED): A type of LED TV that uses quantum dots to enhance color accuracy and brightness. QLED TVs offer wider color gamuts and better HDR performance than standard LED TVs.

OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode): Uses organic materials that emit their own light, resulting in perfect blacks, infinite contrast ratios, and wider viewing angles. OLED TVs offer exceptional picture quality but tend to be more expensive than LED or QLED TVs.

Contrast Ratio and HDR

Contrast Ratio: A key specification that measures the difference between the brightest and darkest parts of an image. A higher contrast ratio indicates better detail in both dark and bright areas, resulting in a more realistic picture.

HDR (High Dynamic Range): A technology that expands the range of colors and brightness levels a TV can display, creating more realistic and vibrant images. HDR TVs offer a wider dynamic range, with more detail in highlights and shadows.
