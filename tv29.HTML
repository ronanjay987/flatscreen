Screen Size and Resolution

Screen size, measured diagonally in inches, determines a TV's physical size. Choose a screen size that complements your room size and viewing distance.

Resolution, referring to the number of pixels on the screen, impacts image clarity and sharpness. Common resolutions include Full HD (1080p), 4K (2160p), and 8K (4320p), with higher resolutions offering greater detail.

Display Technology

LED (Light-Emitting Diode): A widely used technology that uses LEDs to illuminate the backlight. LED TVs offer good picture quality, energy efficiency, and affordability.

QLED (Quantum Dot LED): An advanced LED technology using quantum dots to enhance color accuracy and brightness. QLED TVs deliver wider color gamuts and better HDR performance than standard LED TVs.

OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode): Uses organic materials that emit their own light, resulting in perfect blacks, infinite contrast ratios, and wider viewing angles. OLED TVs offer exceptional picture quality but are typically more expensive.

Contrast Ratio and HDR

Contrast Ratio: Measures the difference between the brightest and darkest parts of an image. A higher contrast ratio indicates better detail in both dark and bright areas, resulting in a more realistic picture.

HDR (High Dynamic Range): A technology that expands the range of colors and brightness levels a TV can display, creating more realistic and vibrant images. HDR TVs offer a wider dynamic range, with more detail in highlights and shadows.
